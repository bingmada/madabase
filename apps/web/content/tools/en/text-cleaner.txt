[FAQ]
What does a text cleaner do?
A text cleaner removes unwanted formatting and normalizes text. Common operations include removing extra spaces, normalizing line breaks, trimming whitespace, and removing special characters.

What cleaning options are available?
Common options include: remove extra spaces, normalize line breaks (Windows to Unix), remove leading/trailing whitespace, remove special characters, and convert tabs to spaces.

Why clean text before database import?
Inconsistent text formatting can cause issues with search, sorting, and display. Cleaning ensures consistent data quality.

Can I remove only extra spaces?
Yes. Choose specific cleaning operations to apply only the transformations you need.

Does it handle line breaks from different systems?
Yes. It can normalize line breaks from Windows (CRLF), Mac (CR), and Unix (LF) formats.

Is my text uploaded?
No. All cleaning happens locally in your browser.
