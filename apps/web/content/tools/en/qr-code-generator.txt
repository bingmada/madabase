QR codes (Quick Response codes) are two-dimensional barcodes that can store various types of data, from URLs and contact information to Wi-Fi credentials and payment details. [FAQ]
What is a QR code?
A QR code (Quick Response code) is a two-dimensional barcode consisting of black and white squares arranged in a grid. It can store various types of data including URLs, text, contact information, and more.

What types of data can I encode?
You can encode URLs, plain text, email addresses (mailto:), phone numbers (tel:), SMS messages, Wi-Fi credentials, vCards, and calendar events.

What is the difference between PNG and SVG?
PNG is a raster image format suitable for web use and general purposes. SVG is a vector format that can be scaled infinitely without losing quality, making it ideal for print.

What is error correction?
QR codes include error correction that allows them to be scanned even if partially damaged or obscured. Higher error correction levels provide more resilience but require more space.

How do I scan a QR code?
Use your smartphone camera app or a QR code scanner app. Point the camera at the QR code, and most modern phones will automatically detect and decode it.

Why is my QR code not working?
Ensure the content is not too long (QR codes have capacity limits), the contrast between black and white is clear, and there is sufficient quiet zone (margin) around the code.

Can I customize the QR code colors?
This basic version generates black and white QR codes. For colored versions, you may need specialized QR code software.

What size should I use?
For digital use, 200-400 pixels is usually sufficient. For print, ensure at least 300 DPI at the intended print size.
